About Me

I am a post-doctoral scientist at the International Institute of Molecular and Cell Biology (IIMCB) in Warsaw, Poland, specializing in computational structural biology. My research focuses on developing and applying computational methods to understand the complex relationship between RNA sequence, structure, and function.

I completed my PhD at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur in 2018, where I investigated the molecular mechanisms of RNA–protein interactions based on their physicochemical properties. This work laid the foundation for my current interests in applying machine learning to structural biology and developing computational tools for the scientific community.

My interdisciplinary approach blends computer science, biophysics, and molecular biology to address challenging problems in RNA research. I am passionate about building accessible tools that advance discoveries in structural biology.

Research Interests

  • RNA structure prediction & modeling (actively involved in RNA-Puzzles and CASP)
  • Integrative modeling of biomolecular structures with cryo-EM maps
  • Developing tools for macromolecular structures and interactions
